Coronavirus (Covid-19) information for Members of the Public

Can I still submit a request for my application to be expedited?

Yes. You can still request expedition of your application. We may not be able to give an accurate completion time as our caseworker resource is reduced due to the current coronavirus (COVID-19) situation, but we will aim to process your application as a priority.

Will HM Land Registry give more time in which to respond to notices?

Yes, where we can. If customers are concerned that they will be unable to respond to notices due to COVID-19 we will look favourably on any request to extend or allow a further period for making an objection. However, there are a number of circumstances where we cannot offer an extension, the most commonly encountered include where:

  • the relevant rule under the Land Registration Rules 2003 prescribes a period or a maximum period and whilst this maximum period itself cannot be extended, we may be able to delay determining the application concerned to allow for late responses. An example of where we could do this would be to allow for a late objection to an application for the entry of a restriction (s.45(2) and r.92(9)).
  • the registrar serves notice of an application to cancel a caution against first registration (s.18(4) and r.53) or to cancel a unilateral notice (s.36(3) and r.86) on the expiry of the prescribed period, which cannot be extended beyond a maximum number of days, the registrar is obliged to cancel the caution or unilateral notice in the absence of an objection.
  • The period for serving counter notice to an application for registration as proprietor by a squatter under Schedule 6 to the LRA 2002 cannot be extended (para.3 of Sch.6 and r.189). Counter-notice is ineffective once the notice period has expired: paragraphs 3(2) and 4 of Schedule 6. Rule 189 deals with the notice period for giving counter-notice: no provision is made for the period to be extended.

I am unable to visit HMLR in person how can I have my identity verified and submit my application to HMLR?

From 18 March 2020 we temporarily suspended our service that allows customers to make appointments to visit HM Land Registry in person.

Customers can no longer visit one of our offices to have their identity verified or submit an application for registration with us in person. We sincerely apologise for any inconvenience this causes. We are following current government guidance on slowing the further spread of COVID-19 and will update our GOV.UK pages once the service is available again.

Customers who were planning to visit one of our offices to have their identity verified using Form ID1, or Form ID2, and personally submit an application for registration with us will temporarily be unable to do so. We advise that you thoroughly check that you need to include an ID1 form with your application. You can have your identity verified and the form signed by any of the professionals listed on Form ID1, if available.

From Monday 4th May we have made some temporary changes to make it easier to have your identity verified. This includes extending the professions of people who can verify identity and allowing verification to be carried out by video call where certain conditions are met.

Further details on these changes are available in our new practice guide 67A: temporary changes to HM Land Registry’s evidence of identity requirements.

On 26th May minor amendments to practice guide 67A were made as a result of customer feedback. Some changes have also been made to the evidence of identity forms referred to in the guide, in particular to forms ID1, ID2 and ID5 in relation to verification by way of a video call.

Applications can still be submitted by post to: HM Land Registry Citizen Centre PO Box 74 Gloucester GL14 9BB

In the meantime, we are conscious of the difficulties in obtaining identity verification in the current circumstances. So, we have decided to stop rejecting applications where evidence or confirmation of identity is completely missing until further notice. We expect customers, however, to complete applications as correctly and as fully as possible. We will be raising a requisition where the information is missing or insufficient

Is there anything we can do to make the lives of the HMLR employees any easier?

Please review online guidance notes before contacting the Customer Support Centre. Please be assured that we will not cancel applications without warning.

What arrangements have been made for lodging of First Registration applications in the current situation? Are we able to continue lodging these by post?

We are able to accept first registration applications lodged by post in the usual way. We appreciate that there may be some difficulties in lodging or certifying all deeds and documents required in support of an application. If you have knowingly sent us an incomplete application, you must send us the missing or updated information as soon as it is available. You do not need to wait for us to send you a requisition.

Have any arrangements been made to facilitate alternative methods of execution during this unprecedented time?

From Monday 4 May we have made temporary changes to make it easier to sign documents for registration purposes. We will accept deeds that have been signed using the ‘Mercury signing approach’.

This means that, for land registration purposes, a signature page will need to be signed in pen and witnessed in person (not by a video call). The signature will then need to be captured, with a scanner or a camera, to produce a PDF, JPEG or other suitable copy of the signed signature page. Each party sends a single email to their conveyancer to which is attached the final agreed copy of the document and the copy of the signed signature page.

Practice guide 8: execution of deeds has been updated with more information. has been updated with more information.

What is the definition of a dispositionary deed for the purpose of a Mercury signature? Does it include a mortgage deed?

S27(2) and (3) of the Land Registration Act 2002 lists the “registrable dispositions”. A dispositionary deed will be a deed that effects one of these dispositions. As it includes the grant of a legal charge, it includes a mortgage deed.

If lenders have not changed their requirements for execution, can mortgage deeds be executed using the Mercury signing process?

All parties in a transaction will need to agree to using the Mercury signing process. If a lender is not prepared to use this process, then the usual method of execution should be used.
